## Executive Summary

- **Device code / OAuth abuse is now an industrial-scale attack vector.** EvilTokens PhaaS and the AI-enabled device code phishing campaign documented by Microsoft both exploit the OAuth 2.0 Device Authorization Grant flow — a technique that bypasses MFA *architecturally*, not just practically. With device code phishing surging 37× in 2026 and EvilTokens compressing token-to-BEC-wire-fraud to under 15 minutes via LLM automation, this is the most operationally urgent threat facing M365 tenants in April 2026.

- **AiTM session hijacking remains the dominant PhaaS delivery mechanism against M365.** Despite the March 2026 law-enforcement takedown of Tycoon2FA (Storm-1747), the "Breaking the Code" AiTM campaign hit 35,000+ users across 13,000 organizations within 72 hours in mid-April — demonstrating that PhaaS infrastructure resilience now outpaces law-enforcement disruption timelines. Non-phishing-resistant MFA is the primary enabler for every documented AiTM chain.

- **Nation-state actors (APT28 / Forest Blizzard) now operate M365 credential theft at the network infrastructure layer.** The FrostArmada campaign compromised 18,000+ SOHO routers across 120 countries to silently intercept Microsoft 365 authentication flows — entirely bypassing tenant-level controls including MFA and Conditional Access. The FBI's April 2026 disruption is a tactical win, not a strategic one; the TTP remains available to any actor with SOHO-exploit capability.

- **AI-augmented BEC automation has eliminated the manual kill-chain bottleneck.** EvilTokens integrates Meta Llama 3.1/3.3 (via Groq API) to automatically mine stolen mailboxes for payment threads and generate hyper-personalized BEC fraud emails within minutes of initial compromise — removing the reconnaissance and lure-crafting steps that previously limited BEC throughput and required skilled operators.

- **Phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2 / passkeys / CBA) is the single control that defeats every major April 2026 campaign technique.** AiTM proxy chains, QR code phishing, CAPTCHA-gated PhaaS, and DNS-layer credential interception were all designed to defeat TOTP/SMS MFA. Organizations that have enforced FIDO2 or certificate-based authentication were architecturally protected against all documented April 2026 primary TTPs. For device code phishing, restricting the Device Authorization Grant flow in Conditional Access is the required companion control.
