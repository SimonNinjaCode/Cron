# M365 Threat Intelligence Report — 2026-05-22

**Target Month:** April 2026 | **Compiled:** 2026-05-22 | **Audience:** Security Architects · SOC Leads · Cyber Leadership

---

## Executive Summary

- **Device code phishing is now an industrial-scale attack class.** Five Russia-aligned actors (Storm-2372, APT29, UTA0304, UTA0307, UNK_AcademicFlare) collectively compromised 340+ Microsoft 365 organizations in Q1 2026. The emergence of the EvilTokens PhaaS platform and Tycoon2FA's post-disruption resurgence signal that blocking one operator has no lasting effect — the capability has been commoditized at scale.

- **Entra ID's identity plane is the most contested terrain in enterprise security.** April 2026 alone produced a novel privilege-escalation flaw (Agent ID Administrator role → service principal takeover), a payroll-fraud AiTM campaign (Storm-2755 targeting Canadian M365 users), and continued SSPR abuse (Storm-2949). Each represents a distinct identity attack surface; patching one does not close the others.

- **Microsoft's Q1 2026 email threat volume reached 8.3 billion attempts.** QR code phishing grew 146% quarter-over-quarter; CAPTCHA-gated lures more than doubled in March. Traditional email security controls — link scanning, attachment sandboxing — are being systematically bypassed by evolving delivery and evasion techniques.

- **Nation-state actors are layering network-layer and identity-layer attacks.** Forest Blizzard (Russian GRU) compromised 5,000+ SOHO routers in April 2026 to hijack DNS and intercept Microsoft Outlook credentials via AiTM — combining infrastructure compromise with cloud identity theft in a single kill chain.

- **Patch Tuesday April 2026 was the second-largest in Microsoft history (167 flaws, 2 zero-days).** CVE-2026-32201 (SharePoint Server spoofing, actively exploited) and CVE-2026-33825 (Microsoft Defender EoP) both affect core M365 and endpoint protection components, underscoring that vulnerability management cadence must keep pace with threat actor exploitation speed.
